# Time-Series Analytics Report

## Revenue Trends
Revenue increased significantly over the 12-month period, rising from **$217,845 in April 2025** to **$1,708,515 in December 2025**. The strongest growth occurred during **October to November 2025**, indicating a major acceleration in sales during late Q4.

After this peak, revenue dropped to **$729,311 in January 2026**, which may reflect a seasonal slowdown or weaker post-holiday demand. Performance then recovered in **March 2026**, when revenue reached **$1,530,931**, showing that the decline was temporary rather than a sustained downturn.

## Customer Retention
Customer retention analysis showed that the **December 2025** and **February 2026** cohorts had the strongest retention performance, meaning customers acquired in those months were more likely to return and make repeat purchases. In contrast, the **April 2025 cohort** had the weakest retention, suggesting lower customer quality or weaker post-purchase engagement during that period.

Across most cohorts, repeat purchases became more noticeable by the **90-day retention window**, which suggests that many customers did not return immediately but were more likely to make another purchase over a longer period.


## Category Performance
Product category analysis showed that **Electronics** and **Home** delivered the strongest revenue growth over the past 12 months, while **Apparel** remained relatively stable. This suggests that demand expansion was driven mainly by the higher-growth categories, while Apparel contributed as a more consistent but less dynamic category.

This category mix indicates that the business may benefit from allocating more marketing and inventory resources toward categories with stronger growth momentum.

## Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the business should prioritize the following actions next quarter:

1. Focus marketing efforts on the high-retention cohorts, especially **December 2025** and **February 2026**, to better understand and replicate the conditions that drove stronger repeat purchase behavior.
2. Increase investment in high-growth categories such as **Electronics** and **Home** to maximize revenue expansion.
3. Investigate weaker periods, especially **January 2026**, to determine whether the decline was caused by seasonality, weaker campaigns, or reduced customer demand.
4. Continue monitoring customer behavior across **30-day, 60-day, and 90-day retention windows**, since the analysis suggests that many repeat purchases happen later rather than immediately.
